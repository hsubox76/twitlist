Setup Instructions

There are two parts to Twitlist, the extension, and the website.

  1. Install the extension.

    Download the extension here. You need to be using the Chrome browser.

  2. Find the extension icon.

    Once the extension has been installed, go to twitter.com and then look for this icon in your top bar:

    Note that this icon won't appear if you're on any other website!

  3. Log in to the extension.

    Click on the icon to open a popup. The popup will contain a green button asking you to "sign in with Twitter".

    Do it! A large popup will open to do the login.

    I swear I am not reading your Twitter account. That's the minimal amount of permissions I'm allowed to set the app to by Twitter. All I'm using your account for is to log in and verify you're you. Anyway, when it's done, it'll go away and you should be logged in.

  4. Add a note.

    Every tweet should have a little purple plus icon next to the person's name. When you hover on the plus, a box will appear below it saying "click to add a note about this user".

    Click the plus and the box will turn into a form allowing you to add a new note:

    Type in your note and click "save":

    Now the note should appear on hover, and the plus icon should turn into a list icon, which indicates this is a user you've added a note for.

Manage or Share Your List

  • Logging on to the website.

    To view a list of your notes, delete notes, or share your list with other people, go to the website. (This will open another tab, separate from your Twitter tab.) You can go there by clicking the "manage notes" link in the add/edit note form on each tweet or the "Manage your list (opens web page)" link in the settings.

    This will take you to the website (twitlist.net). If it's your first time, and it should be, you will be asked to log in again.

    Sorry about that, but it can't be helped, a website and an extension are two different things. Going forward you probably won't have to log in again, unless you deliberately log out.

  • Editing and deleting notes.

    The first section should be a list of your notes. Click "edit" or "delete" next to any note to edit or delete it.

  • Setting sharing options.

    Lists are set to private by default, which means no one else can see your list. At the bottom, it should say, "This list can be seen by" and the selected option should be "nobody". The options are:

    • nobody

      This is the default.

    • certain users (listed below)

      You can share the list with other users by adding them in the "share with" box at the bottom of this section. You need to use their Twitter handle and they need to have registered with Twitlist. If they haven't, you can still add their Twitter handle and they'll be eligible to see your shared list as soon as they sign up.

    • everyone

      >You'll get a public link to share this list with anyone who visits that link.

Troubleshooting

  • Nothing happens when I click the login button?

    The button triggers a popup that lets you log in with your Twitter account. If you have an ad blocker or browser ad-blocking settings it may be blocking the login popup. See if you can change your settings to allow popups from this page.

  • I'm not very familiar with installing/updating/deleting Chrome Extensions.

    Here is the help page for installing and managing Chrome Extensions. In addition to navigating to the extensions settings through the menu, you can also type

    chrome://extensions

    into the URL bar to go straight there.

  • Where can I report bugs?

    Uh... let's try the Github issues page for this repo or DM me on Twitter if you know my handle.

Other FAQ

  • What is the purpose of this?

    Honestly, I've seen a lot of people retweet very bad people because they've forgotten who that person is. However, this can be used for many positive purposes such as:

    1. Remembering why you followed someone.
    2. Remembering who someone is (journalist? comedian?) despite their useless whimsical bio.
    3. Telling two similar people apart.
    4. Keeping track of people who keep screwing around with holiday themed names.
    5. Sharing useful info about who someone is, or why they are famous, with your friends.

  • What do I need to run this?

    1. Chrome (desktop): This extension only works if/when you are browsing Twitter in the Chrome browser, on desktop. It won't work on the Twitter iPhone or Android apps, and it won't work in the Chrome browser on a phone.
    2. To create and save a list, you will need a Twitter account.

  • Can I just sign up on the website and not use the extension?

    Sure, signing up on the website lets you look at other people's lists they've shared with you. Anyone can look at public lists ("shared with everyone") without having an account.

    There's no UI to manually add entries if you're not coming from the extension but you could do it by messing around with the URL params. May add a UI for this in the future.

  • Can we set only certain entries to be private?

    I added that functionality but it's very tricky and I'm afraid something may go wrong. Maybe after all the existing bugs have been worked out!

  • Can we create multiple lists?

    That seems like a lot of trouble.

  • What data are you collecting??

    I'm collecting your twitter username and linking it to your twitlist account. I am also collecting all the notes you make on this app, obviously. I am not reading anything on your Twitter account. I know the login says I have access to your tweets and DMs but despite the fact I only want to use Twitter login verification and get your screenname, reading your account is the minimal amount of permissions a Twitter-integrated app can ask for.
